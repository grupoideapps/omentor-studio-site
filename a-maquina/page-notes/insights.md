# Page Insights

## Product lockup

- The product mark works best as a three-line stacked composition.
- The top line is the smallest and lightest.
- The middle line is the main brand anchor and should stay the strongest typographic element.
- The bottom line carries the CTA-like emphasis and pairs with the icon.
- The lockup must stay transparent and avoid any filled background.
- Keep the internal icon proportional to the type block, not to the outer page width.

## Layout behavior

- The hero uses the lockup above the main headline.
- The price block reuses the same lockup with a smaller footprint.
- The lockup should scale through its own width token, not by changing the page grid.
- Vertical density matters more than extra padding.

## Typography

- Use the design system font stack only.
- Preserve uppercase treatment.
- Keep line breaks explicit and stable.

## Asset Strategy

These images are not decorative. Each one has a job in the persuasion flow of the LP.

| Asset | Strategic function | What the image should communicate |
|-------|--------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Hero before | Contrast and pain state | The persona before the product: stuck, disorganized, frustrated, unclear |
| Hero after | Promise and outcome | The persona after the product: calm, clear, resolved, with visible improvement |
| Testimonial | Proof | A real result from someone like the reader, shown as a screenshot or testimonial card |
| Rumination | Identification | The thoughts the persona repeats at the peak of the pain, visually fragmented and emotional |
| Step image | Mechanism | One clear step of the method, simple enough to feel doable |
| Product mockup | Tangibility | The actual deliverable in a clean mockup, so the offer feels concrete |
| Transformation | Future state | The change the product creates, not the product itself |
| All mockups together | Value stack | The full package assembled, dense and complete |
| Author photo | Authority | A real face that makes the creator feel credible and human |

## Logo Brief

- Treat the product lockup as a logo system, not as a decorative badge.
- The wordmark stays as three stacked lines, but the icon should be semantically tied to the product.
- Build the icon as a new inline SVG, inspired by Iconify's icon language: simple geometry, clear silhouette, consistent stroke or fill logic, and small-size legibility.
- Avoid using a generic star unless the product concept truly calls for it.
- Think like a brand designer: pick the strongest noun or metaphor from the product category and reduce it to a clean glyph.
- The same icon must work in hero and price contexts without changing the core mark.
